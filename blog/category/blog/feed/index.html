What is a KUB Ultrasound?

KUB ultrasound functions as a particular imaging test that targets the evaluation of the kidney along with the ureter and the bladder region. The procedure bounces sound waves within body organs before exhibiting pictures live on a monitor. The ultrasound procedure offers safety benefits to children and those who are pregnant since it avoids radiation exposure which CT scan and MRI technology employs.

What Is the USG PUB Test Used For?

The USG PUB test can be very useful in identifying the problems present in the urinary system. The commonly utilized KUB ultrasound tests include the following conditions:

]]> What Are the Different Types of Diabetes Tests? https://precisionpathlab.com/blog/different-types-of-diabetes-tests/ Mon, 16 Dec 2024 06:59:54 +0000 https://precisionpathlab.com/blog/?p=183 Diabetes serves as a chronic health situation that showcases how your body converts food into energy. And, if not treated, it might result in serious health issues, which include kidney failure, heart disease, and vision loss. Varying tests are also available that help in the identification and monitoring of diabetes, each with various processes and purposes.

What are Diabetes Tests?

Hence, diabetes tests are diagnostic tools that are taken is used to measure blood glucose levels in your body. And, all tests help to determine whether an individual has prediabetes, diabetes, or gestational diabetes. Moreover, tests also help in monitoring blood sugar levels in the body who are already diagnosed with this condition. Just by identifying certain issues in glucose levels, all these tests allow healthcare professionals to design efficient treatment plans while recommending various lifestyle adjustments.

 List of Blood Tests That Help Identify Diabetes

Diabetes tests help monitor blood sugar levels and detect prediabetes or diabetes early. Here’s a list of essential blood tests to diagnose diabetes effectively

  • Fasting Plasma Glucose (FPG) Test
  • Random Plasma Glucose (RPG) Test
  • Postprandial Blood Sugar Test (PPBS)
  • HbA1C Test
  • Oral Glucose Tolerance Test (OGTT)

Fasting Plasma Glucose (FPG) Test

The best test that effectively measures blood sugar levels after a person has fasted for almost 8 hours. This test is usually used to diagnose prediabetes and diabetes. Fasting plasma glucose test includes drawing off a blood sample from a vein, usually in the morning before breakfast. And, this test is highly effective in diagnosing diabetes during early stages. Moreover, in this test blood sugar values include:

  • Normal – Less than 100mg/dL.
  • Prediabetes – 100-125 mg/dL.
  • Diabetes – 126 mg/dL or more.

Random Plasma Glucose (RPG) Test

This test effectively measures blood sugar levels at any time during the day, irrespective of when an individual last ate. In this test, a diagnostic centre draws blood samples without any prior fasting. The random plasma glucose test is usually used to properly assess your body’s glucose levels showing diabetes symptoms. Hence, its blood sugar values include:

  • Normal – Less than 140 mg/dL.
  • Diabetes – 200 mg/dL or more, along with certain symptoms like frequent urination or lots of thirst.

Postprandial Blood Sugar Test (PPBS)

Postprandial blood sugar test effectively measures blood sugar levels only two hours after meal. This test needs an individual to eat a meal and then have to check their blood glucose exactly after two hours. Moreover, this test properly evaluates how your body manages blood sugar just after eating and could easily detect insulin resistance. Moreover, its blood sugar values are:

  • Normal – Less than 140 mg/dL.
  • Prediabetes – 140-199 mg/dL.
  • Diabetes – 200 mg/dL or more.

HbA1C Test

Well-known as a glycated hemoglobin test, the HbA1C test properly measures average blood sugar levels over the past 3 months. In this test, a blood sample is already drawn, and the test calculates the percentage of glucose combined with hemoglobin in red blood cells. And, this test monitors long-term diabetes and diagnoses the effectiveness of treatment plans. And, its blood sugar levels include:

  • Normal – Less than 5.7%.
  • Prediabetes – Ranges between 5.7-6.4%.
  • Diabetes – 6.5% or more.

Oral Glucose Tolerance Test (OGTT)

The Oral Glucose Tolerance Test effectively evaluates your body’s ability to process glucose just after consumption of sugary beverages. And, this test includes fasting the whole night, drinking glucose solution, and measuring blood sugar levels at frequent intervals. And, this effective test is used in diagnosing gestational diabetes during pregnancy and it diagnoses glucose tolerance. And, its blood sugar levels after 2 hours of glucose drink are:

  • Normal – Less than 140 mg/dL.
  • Prediabetes – 140-199 mg/dL.
  • Diabetes – 200 mg/dL or more.

Key Signs It’s Time to Visit a Diagnostic Center

It is necessary to consult a diagnostic center for diabetes testing if you experience certain symptoms:

  • Enhanced hunger or thirst.
  • Inadequate weight loss.
  • Frequent urination.
  • Irritation.
  • Fatigue.
  • Blurry vision.
  • Slow healing wounds.

Moreover, you should schedule various routine tests if you possess various risk factors, like a family history of diabetes, a sedentary lifestyle, obesity, and high blood pressure. And, pregnant women are recommended to undergo diabetes testing during the 24th-28th week of their pregnancy, as it rules out gestational diabetes.

Some medical conditions like kidney stones, liver problems, diabetes, metabolic disorders, cancer, etc. also influence urine color. Not only in color but some symptoms like painful urination, peeing more often, etc. are observed due to the effects of medicines on our body.

    Detailed Breakdown of Urine Color Chart

    Clear or Transparent

    Urine color is turned into clear or transparent like water due to ‘excess of water intake and minimal waste products present in urine’ in our body. Clear color is usually a positive sign of hydration, but it’s essential to maintain a balance and not overhydrate, which can also have potential health risks.

    Pale Yellow

    Pale yellow is typically considered a sign of good ‘hydration and overall health’ in our body. This color shows adequate hydration, a balanced diet, effective functioning of kidneys, and efficient intake of vitamins and nutrients. Sometimes yellow color of urine is because of the rich B vitamins in the body.

    Transparent Yellow

    A change in the transparent yellow color of urine is a sign of ‘optimal hydration’ which indicates that the body’s intake of fluids is more than its need for basic function. This change is not harmful to the body. It can be the result of consuming food or drinks with natural diuretic properties, such as alcohol and it may be due to climate change, medication, and individual variations.

    Dark Yellow

    A dark yellow color indicates ‘moderate dehydration’ in our body. It shows that the need for water is more than the intake in our body for functioning well. It may be the result of environmental factors like hot weather, high altitudes, and intense physical activity, which turns urine color to dark yellow. To cure this a normal need of ‘8 glasses of water’ should be fulfilled to make the body properly hydrated.

    Amber or Honey

    The urine color of honey gives hints about ’moderate to severe dehydration’ in our body. It can be caused by loses of more fluid through sweating, breathing, and urination in our body than intake water. With honey urine color some other factors like extreme thirst, fatigue, dizziness, and decreased urine output are observed in the body. It may be due to liver diseases, kidney stones, and urinary tract infections.

    Dark Yellow or Orange

    The dark yellow or orange color of urine is sign of ‘dehydration to underlying health conditions’ in our body. Many factors are affecting pee color to orange like medication, dehydration, liver conditions, bile duct obstruction, urinary tract infections, hematuria, metabolic disorder, supplements of vitamins B etc. Sometimes it is result of certain food like carrots, beets, artificial food dyes etc. This change should be noticed and consulted to doctor before more severe problem in body.

    Pink or Red

    The pink or red color in urine can be alarming and should be investigated promptly. It can be caused by factors like blood in urine, medications (antibiotics or senna), beets or blackberries, menstruation in women, porphyria, kidney stones, injury, enlarged prostate, glomerulonephritis, cancer, and strenuous exercise. The effects of this change should be treated at the right time.

    Blue or Green

    Blue or green color in urine is less common than pink or red, but it can still be a cause for concern. Some reasons for this color are like food or medical tests dyes, bilirubin, genetic disorder, pseudomonas infection, methyl blue poisoning, altered Ph level, and due to supplements like vitamin B2. It is harmless and temporary but need to be proper evaluation and diagnosis. However, other symptoms are also detected with it like pain, fever, or change in urinary habits.

    Dark Brown

    The dark brown color in urine indicates various health issues. Caused factors are dehydration, liver disorder, bile duct obstruction, hematuria, Rhabdomyolysis, Hemolytic Anemia, infection diseases, Alkaptonuria, toxic injection, kidney stones, and Autoimmune disorders. Depending upon the suspected underlying cause, further testing, including urine analysis, blood tests, or other diagnostic procedures.

    Cloudy or Murky

    Cloudy or murky urine may also indicate the presence of protein, blood, or pus in the urine, which could be a sign of a more serious underlying condition such as kidney disease, urinary tract infections, or bladder infections. It is important to pay attention to any accompanying symptoms like pain, burning, sensation during urination, fever, or frequent urination, and seek medical attention promptly if you experience any concerning changes in your urine.
