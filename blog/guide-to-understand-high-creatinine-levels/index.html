    Is Your Creatinine Level High? Discover the Symptoms, Causes, and Treatment

    Creatinine is thus an extra product that is generated as a by-product of the catabolism of muscle tissue. Creatinine is one of the wastes which cleared from the body by the working of the kidneys where it is filtered from the blood and then passed through the urine.

    Creatinine is produced when the kidney is in poor health and if there is a high concentration of creatinine in blood then it means that the kidney is not healthy. It should be necessary to have good kidney and general health to understand the manifestations, factors, and management regarding high creatinine levels.

    What is Creatinine?

    Creatinine is the result of the metabolism of creatine this being a component of muscles that is subject to catabolism. Creatine is used for supplying energy to muscles and during this process, creatinine is produced. At other times, through the urinal system, creatinine is removed from the blood through the kidneys.

    The Creatinine blood test is one of those tests that are used in the assessment of renal health. It is believed that relatively high RTL levels indicate that kidneys do not meet the required performance.

    Normal Creatinine Levels by Age

    Average creatinine clearance may also differ depending on age, sex, muscle bulk, and level of health. Here is a Normal Creatinine Levels by age:

    Children: Typically between 0. 2 and 0. 7 mg/dL. If this is the case, levels would be slightly different depending on the childs age and muscular development.

    Adult Women: On average, ranging from 0. 5 and 1. 1 mg/dL. Due to the physiologic differences, women usually have lesser muscle bulk than men, contributing to lower creatinine levels.

    Adult Men: Generally, it is not more than 0. 6 and 1. 2 mg/dL. This is usually traced to the fact that, on average, men have higher muscle mass. Thus, their creatinine level will be slightly higher, too.

    Elderly Individuals: Results Creatinine levels are slightly higher than expected mainly due to reduced muscle mass and aging effect on kidney function.

    Symptoms of High Creatinine Level

    Some cases may bother the patient, but they are not related to high creatinine levels but other illnesses the patient might be having; the following are some symptoms of Creatinine Level High that may appear:

    Fatigue and Weakness: If one has aches and pains, then one may have a kidney disease, or if he or she feels weak and tired.

    Swelling (Edema): Bloating, particularly of the hands, feet, or legs is the general sign of poor kidneys.

    Changes in Urine Output: Reduction of the number of urinations per day or changes in the color, consistency, or odor of urine.

    Nausea and Vomiting: It is also worthwhile to point out that some kinds of digestive disturbances may also be manifested during the progression of moderate renal dysfunction.

    Shortness of Breath: This is termed pulmonary edema which means increased fluid content in the lungs which may lead to breathing difficulties.

    Confusion or Difficulty Concentrating: Dementia may also present due to the accumulation of toxins in the body abnormalities may culminate in impairment of higher mental function.

    Causes of Creatinine Level High

    Factors that cause high creatinine levels include:

    Kidney-related Disease: Kidney diseases are like chronic kidney disease or acute kidney injury which raise the creatinine levels in the body.

    Dehydration: They also noted that if people consume small quantities of water in a day, then the creatinine will be higher because less blood flows to the kidneys.

    Muscle Breakdown: Other factors that can raise creatinine levels include diseases like rhabdomyolysis, where muscle tissue is rapidly broken down.

    Obstructions: Problems which include kidney stones and enlarged prostate glands, affect the working functions of the kidneys.

    Medications: The following drugs are known to have adverse effects on the kidneys: NSAIDs, certain antibiotic drugs, and fenprostalol.

    Treatment of High Creatinine Level

    Treatment of Creatinine Level High involves the management of its cause and kidney care if the kidneys cause the high production of creatinine. Approaches include:

    Hydration: Yes, if a person is dehydrated, then there is a chance that high levels of creatinine are due to it, and in that case, merely by increasing fluid intake, the situation can be handled.

    Medication Adjustment: Ensure the change of medicines likely to affect the kidney is checked and altered accordingly if necessary.

    Dietary modifications: Decreasing the protein intake per day and not using salt or limiting the intake of salt can also bring less stress to the kidneys.

    Managing some Conditions: One should know various ways to protect from new cases of such diseases as diabetes and hypertension so that the kidneys of a patient can be saved.

    Dialysis: Sometimes when the kidneys are impaired in their functioning the patient will need to undergo dialysis to clear wastes in the body.
